When Does Processing Speed Up or Slow Down?

Fastest Processing

November to June. Low filing volumes, shorter CPC queue. Daily throughput typically 2-5 lakh. Best time to expect quick refunds.

Slowest Processing

August to October. Post-July 31 deadline rush. Crores of returns flood CPC simultaneously. Daily throughput can spike to 7 lakh but queue is huge.

Weekdays

Normal processing. CPC runs Monday to Friday. Expect steady daily increments of 2-7 lakh on working days.

Weekends & Holidays

Near-zero processing. Government offices closed. Your return simply waits in queue - it doesn't go backward.

How Processing Speed Affects Your Refund

Once your return is verified, it enters CPC's queue. The time from verification to "Processed" status depends entirely on where you are in that queue and how fast CPC is working through it.

If CPC processes 5 lakh returns per day and 50 lakh returns are ahead of yours in the queue, you're looking at roughly 10 days just for your turn to come. Add processing time (1-2 days per return) and refund initiation (3-7 days), and you have your timeline.

Methodology - How We Track This Data

We check the Income Tax e-Filing portal daily and record the cumulative statistics displayed on the portal's public dashboard. The daily processing figure is calculated as today's cumulative count minus yesterday's count. Weekends and holidays naturally show near-zero daily additions since CPC doesn't process on non-working days. All data is from the official portal and represents the government's own published numbers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many ITRs are processed per day?
During active processing periods, CPC typically processes 2-7 lakh ITRs per day. Peak days (weekdays in Aug-Oct) can see 6-7 lakh. Weekends show near-zero processing.
How long does ITR processing take?
Most simple salaried returns (ITR-1) are processed within 15-30 days of e-verification during off-peak months. Peak season (Aug-Oct) extends this to 45-90 days.
What does "ITRs Processed" mean?
"ITRs Processed" are returns where CPC has completed its assessment - income verified, TDS matched against Form 26AS, and tax liability computed. Each processed return results in a refund, demand, or No Demand No Refund.
Why does processing show zero on weekends?
CPC processes returns on working days only. Weekend and holiday counts show near-zero or zero daily additions. Your return doesn't move backward - it simply waits for the next working day.
Where does this data come from?
We record the cumulative statistics displayed on the official Income Tax e-Filing portal dashboard daily. This is the government's own published data - we just track it over time and calculate daily deltas.
