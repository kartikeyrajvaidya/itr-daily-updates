## Data Posts (Deep & Stat-Heavy)

### Post 3 — The income bracket earthquake
**Insight:** 1.85 crore people left the ≤₹5L bracket in a single year.
**Source:** IT Dept portal — Category wise filing, FY2023-24 vs FY2024-25
```
Something dramatic happened between FY2023-24 and FY2024-25.

Individuals declaring income ≤₹5 lakh:
FY2022-23: 4.65 Cr  (72% of all filers)
FY2023-24: 5.66 Cr  (70.7% of all filers)
FY2024-25: 3.81 Cr  (44.5% of all filers)

1.85 crore people left the ≤₹5L bracket in a single year.

They didn't disappear. The ₹5L-10L bracket
swelled from 1.38 Cr → 3.37 Cr (+19.8 Lakh people).

India's middle income tier just doubled in one year.

Source: IT Dept portal | itrstats.in/insights/
#IncomeTax #IndiaFinance
```

### Post 4 — ITR-2 is the fastest growing form. By far.
**Insight:** ITR-2 (capital gains, multiple income) up 34.69% in one year. 53% in two years.
**Source:** IT Dept portal — Filing Count, FY2022-23 to FY2024-25
```
ITR-2 is for people with capital gains,
multiple properties, or foreign income.

Filings:
FY2022-23: 80.0 lakh
FY2023-24: 91.1 lakh  (+13.85%)
FY2024-25: 1.23 Cr    (+34.69%)

2-year growth: +53.3%

ITR-1 (plain salary) grew just 0.54% last year.
ITR-2 grew 34.69%.

More Indians are investing in mutual funds,
selling property, earning dividends.
The complexity of Indian income is rising fast.

Source: IT Dept portal | itrstats.in/insights/
#ITR #MutualFunds #IndiaFinance
```

### Post 5 — The ITR-1 flatline
**Insight:** Pure salaried filer growth dropped from 9.49% to 0.54% in one year.
**Source:** IT Dept portal — Filing Growth, FY2022-23 to FY2024-25
```
ITR-1 is filed by salaried employees with
simple income — the backbone of the taxpayer base.

Filings:
FY2022-23: 3.29 Cr
FY2023-24: 3.60 Cr  (+9.49%)
FY2024-25: 3.62 Cr  (+0.54%)

Near-zero growth after years of double digits.

Two possible reads:
→ Salaried class has hit a ceiling as a filer base
→ ITR-2 is eating into ITR-1 as more people invest

Both are probably true.

Source: IT Dept portal | itrstats.in/insights/
#ITR #IncomeTax #Salary
```

### Post 6 — 4.82 crore ghost accounts
**Insight:** 4.82 crore people registered on IT portal but never filed.
**Source:** IT Dept portal — Highlights, FY2024-25
```
As of March 2025:

Registered on the IT portal: 14.01 Cr
Actually filed ITR:            9.19 Cr

Gap: 4.82 crore people.

That's more than the entire population of
Colombia or Argentina — registered,
but never filed a single return.

Reasons vary: below threshold,
NRIs, HUFs, deceased accounts.

But 4.82 crore is a staggering number.

Source: IT Dept portal | itrstats.in/insights/
#IncomeTax #ITR
```

### Post 8 — India has 10,184 people who declare income above ₹10 crore
**Insight:** The full income pyramid — from 3.81 Cr at the bottom to 10,184 at the very top.
**Source:** IT Dept portal — Category wise filing, Individual, FY2024-25
```
Of 8.57 crore individual ITR filers in FY2024-25:

≤₹5 lakh:      3.81 Cr  (44.5%)
₹5L – ₹10L:    3.37 Cr  (39.3%)
₹10L – ₹50L:   1.30 Cr  (15.1%)
₹50L – ₹1Cr:   6,40,659  (0.7%)
₹1Cr – ₹5Cr:   2,97,086  (0.35%)
₹5Cr – ₹10Cr:    16,797  (0.02%)
Above ₹10 Cr:     10,184  (0.01%)

India has 140 crore people.
10,184 of them declared income above ₹10 crore.

That's 0.0007% of the population.
```

### Post 9 — ₹4.35 lakh crore in refunds. In one year.
**Insight:** Total refunds issued dropped 26% in FY23-24 then recovered. Most people don't know this number.
**Source:** IT Dept portal — Highlights, FY2022-23 to FY2024-25
```
Total refunds issued by the IT department:

FY2022-23: ₹4,92,190 Cr
FY2023-24: ₹3,61,765 Cr  (dropped 26%)
FY2024-25: ₹4,35,008 Cr  (recovered)

₹4,35,008 crore = ₹4.35 lakh crore returned
to taxpayers in a single financial year.

That's larger than India's entire defence budget.
Twice over.

And yet most people don't know this number exists.

Source: IT Dept portal | itrstats.in/insights/
#ITRRefund #IncomeTax
```
