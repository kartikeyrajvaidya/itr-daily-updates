## What the Market Research Changed

### 1. Calculator-first strategy is validated
AI Overviews have near-zero penetration on tool-intent queries ("X calculator india"). CTR drops 61% when AI Overviews appear — but calculators are structurally exempt. Every hour spent building a calculator is safer than every hour writing a definitional blog post.

### 2. Advance Tax Calculator is more urgent than Capital Gains
March 15 is in 5 days (installment 4 deadline). Miss March 15, still catch June 15, September 15, December 15 — four spikes per year. The SERP is owned by Quora threads. A dedicated calculator ranks easily.

### 3. "Is X lakh salary good" series has a wider opportunity than mapped
Quora and thin-content sites (salaryslip.org, thesalaryslip.com, salaryinhand.in) own the Rs 8L, 15L, 20L variants. These are higher-shareability queries than almost anything else we've planned. The income percentile CBDT angle makes ITR Stats' version structurally different.

### 4. Programmatic salary pages are a new unlock
"15 LPA in hand salary", "20 LPA in hand salary", "25 LPA take home" — dedicated low-DA micro-sites own these. They're beatable. These are essentially thin versions of what our CTC calculator does. 6-8 targeted pages for common LPA amounts could pull 500-1000 visits/month each with minimal effort.

### 5. AIS vs 26AS is a featured snippet target, not a long article
The winning format is a 300-word comparison table, not a 2000-word guide. Quick to build, high-leverage.

### 6. The Warikoo precedent confirms viral mechanics
Ankur Warikoo tweeted income percentile data in January 2024. indiaincomecheck.in (our main competitor) got a viral link that day and has poor SEO despite it. Same concept, different execution. Our calculator is better — it needs distribution, not more building.

### 7. Filing season peak is July 31, 2026
Content must be indexed before June 1 (Google takes 2-4 weeks). We have ~11 weeks to publish and index all filing-season content. The window is tighter than it looks.

## The ITR Stats Moat (Never Lose Sight Of This)

Three things no competitor can replicate quickly:
1. **Live CBDT data** — real filing/processing numbers, updated twice daily
2. **Income percentile from 8.5 crore ITR filings** — the only consumer-facing source
3. **Calculator UX** — dark mode, real-time, side-by-side regime comparison, charts

Every piece of content should either use one of these three things or link to something that does. If a blog post doesn't leverage the moat, it probably shouldn't be built.
