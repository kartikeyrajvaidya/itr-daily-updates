Understanding Your Percentile

If you're in the Top 10%, it means 90% of ITR filers earn less than you.

Remember: Only about 7% of Indians file ITR. If you're filing taxes, you're already in a higher income group compared to the general population.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is income percentile in India?
Income percentile tells you what percentage of ITR filers earn less than you. Being in the top 10% means 90% of all income tax filers in India have a lower income than you.
How many people file ITR in India?
Approximately 8.75 crore (87.5 million) income tax returns are filed annually. This is only about 6-7% of India's total population — most people fall below the taxable income threshold.
What income puts you in the top 1% of ITR filers?
Based on Income Tax Department data, the top 1% of ITR filers earn approximately ₹75 lakh or above annually. The top 2% starts around ₹50 lakh.
Is ITR filer percentile the same as overall population percentile?
No. ITR filers represent only about 7% of India's population. If you file ITR, you're already in a higher income group than most Indians. Being top 50% among ITR filers likely places you in the top 5-7% of all Indians.
What percentage of ITR filers earn less than ₹5 lakh?
Approximately 65% of all ITR filers declare a gross income of ₹5 lakh or below — by far the largest segment of taxpayers in India.
