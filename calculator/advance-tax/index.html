What is Advance Tax and Who Must Pay It

Advance tax is income tax paid in installments during the financial year itself - rather than in a lump sum at the time of filing your ITR. The concept is "pay as you earn." It applies to salaried employees, freelancers, self-employed professionals, and business owners alike.

Who must pay: Under Section 208 of the Income Tax Act, any taxpayer whose estimated tax liability for the year is Rs 10,000 or more - after subtracting TDS already deducted - must pay advance tax.

Who is exempt: Resident senior citizens aged 60 years or above who do NOT have income from business or profession are fully exempt under Section 207. Their tax is settled at ITR filing time. All others - including senior citizens with business income - must comply.

Salaried employees: If your employer deducts sufficient TDS from salary, your advance tax liability may be nil. But if you have income from rent, freelance work, capital gains, or interest on which TDS is not deducted, you need to estimate and pay advance tax on that amount.

Advance Tax Due Dates for FY 2025-26

The advance tax installment schedule under Section 211 has four deadlines. Each deadline is cumulative - meaning by September 15, you must have paid 45% of your total estimated tax, not just 30% more.

InstallmentDue DateCumulative % of Annual Tax Due
1st InstallmentJune 15, 202515%
2nd InstallmentSeptember 15, 202545%
3rd InstallmentDecember 15, 202575%
4th and FinalMarch 15, 2026100%

Exception for presumptive taxpayers: Taxpayers under Section 44AD (business - 8%/6% of turnover) or Section 44ADA (professionals - 50% of gross receipts) pay their entire advance tax in one installment by March 15. No June, September, or December installments required.

How to Calculate Advance Tax - Formula

The calculation follows these steps:

  1. Estimate your total income for the full financial year from all sources
  2. Subtract applicable deductions - under new regime only standard deduction of Rs 75,000; under old regime also 80C, 80D, HRA exemption, home loan interest etc.
  3. Apply income tax slab rates to get base tax. Add surcharge if income exceeds Rs 50 lakh. Add 4% Health and Education Cess.
  4. Subtract TDS that has been or will be deducted during the year.
  5. If the remaining amount is Rs 10,000 or more, that is your advance tax liability. Split it across installments per Section 211 percentages.

Penalties for Late or Short Payment

Missing an advance tax installment or underpaying triggers interest under Sections 234B and 234C. These are not penalties in the conventional sense - they are interest charges on the money the government was owed but not paid on time.

Section 234C - Deferment of Installments: Applies when each quarterly installment is short-paid relative to the cumulative threshold. Interest rate is 1% per month (simple interest).

  • June 15 shortfall: 1% x 3 months on (15% of advance tax - amount paid by Jun 15)
  • September 15 shortfall: 1% x 3 months on (45% cumulative - cumulative paid by Sep 15)
  • December 15 shortfall: 1% x 3 months on (75% cumulative - cumulative paid by Dec 15)
  • March 15 shortfall: 1% x 1 month on (100% - cumulative paid by Mar 15)

Section 234B - Default in Advance Tax Payment: Applies if total advance tax paid before March 31 is less than 90% of your assessed tax. Interest is charged at 1% per month from April 1 of the assessment year until the date of full payment. For FY 2025-26, this would apply from April 1, 2026 onwards if less than 90% was paid.

Example: If your advance tax liability is Rs 1,00,800 and you paid nothing all year, your 234C interest for Q1 shortfall alone = Rs 15,120 x 1% x 3 = Rs 454. Missing all four installments compounds this significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is exempt from advance tax in India? +
Resident senior citizens aged 60 or above who have no income from business or profession are fully exempt from advance tax under Section 207. All other taxpayers with net tax liability of Rs 10,000 or more after TDS must pay advance tax.
When should I pay advance tax for FY 2025-26? +
Advance tax for FY 2025-26 is payable in four installments: 15% by June 15 2025, 45% cumulative by September 15 2025, 75% cumulative by December 15 2025, and 100% by March 15 2026. Presumptive taxpayers under Section 44AD or 44ADA pay the full amount by March 15 only.
What happens if I do not pay advance tax on time? +
Missing an installment triggers Section 234C interest at 1% per month (3 months for June/September/December shortfall, 1 month for March shortfall). If total advance tax paid by March 31 is below 90% of your assessed tax, Section 234B interest accrues from April 1 at 1% per month until full payment. Use the penalty simulator above to calculate your exact liability.
Does TDS reduce my advance tax liability? +
Yes. TDS deducted by your employer or any other payer is credited directly against your total tax liability. Enter your estimated annual TDS in this calculator to see your net advance tax. If TDS already covers 90% or more of your total tax, you do not need to pay advance tax at all.
Do salaried employees need to pay advance tax? +
If your employer deducts sufficient TDS from your salary, your advance tax liability may be nil. However, if you have additional income from rent, capital gains, freelancing, or interest on which TDS is not deducted, you need to estimate that income and pay advance tax on it. Enter just the income on which TDS is not deducted to calculate your liability.
