ITR Processing Weekly Update

Last updated: Feb 28, 2026 (Week of Feb 21 – Feb 27)

February 28, 2026 4 min read

TL;DR

  • Processing has cooled significantly after the mid-Feb burst — only 1.61 lakh returns cleared this week
  • Best single day this week: Feb 23 with +69,501

  • Full clearance now projected mid-April to late May 2026 depending on pace

When Will Everyone Get Their Refund?

Projections have shifted since the mid-February burst faded:

Optimistic (another burst of ~70K/day):

27.6 lakh ÷ 70,000/day = ~39 days → mid-April 2026

Conservative (current pace of ~30K/day continues):

27.6 lakh ÷ 30,000/day = ~92 days → late May 2026

The good news: 23.7 lakh returns have been cleared since early February. The less-good news: at the current pace, the remaining 27.6 lakh will take considerably longer to clear than looked likely two weeks ago.

What To Do If You're Still Waiting

  1. Check your e-verification status - Your return won't be processed until it's verified
  2. Make sure your bank account is pre-validated - This can cause delays at the last step
  3. Check for notices - Login to the e-filing portal and see if there are any pending actions
  4. Be patient - The queue is clearing, just more slowly than mid-February suggested

Pro tip: If it's been more than 60 days since you e-verified and still no refund, raise a grievance on the e-Nivaran portal. With the current moderate pace, don't wait too long.

What We're Watching Next Week

  • Will the IT Department do another burst like Feb 14 or Feb 17, or has pace settled permanently?
  • Can the backlog break below 27 lakh?
  • Will new verifications pick up as the assessment year progresses?

Despite the slowdown, 23.7 lakh returns have been cleared since early February — a remarkable achievement. With 27.6 lakh still pending, the pace of the next few weeks will determine whether refunds arrive in April or push into May. We'll track it daily on the dashboard.
