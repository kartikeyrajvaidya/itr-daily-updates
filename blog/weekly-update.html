When Will Everyone Get Their Refund?

Let's do some quick math:

If the IT Department sustains ~1.4L/day (recent pace):

28.7 lakh ÷ 1.4 lakh/day = ~20 days to clear the entire backlog

That puts us at mid-March 2026 — if no further bursts.

But keep in mind — they've now proven they can do 7 lakh in a single day. If we see even one such burst per week, the backlog could be gone much sooner. The key question is whether Feb 14 was a one-off or part of a pattern.

The Feb 15–16 Pause

Two consecutive days of zero processing after a record burst is worth noting. A few possible explanations:

  • The massive Feb 14 batch run needed time for the portal to recalibrate
  • Weekend processing cycles (Feb 15 = Sunday, Feb 16 = Monday may have had a data lag)
  • System cooldown after a large batch job

This is the same pattern we saw on Feb 13 — a huge processing day followed by a pause. It suggests the IT Department is doing bulk batch runs rather than a smooth daily flow. Either way, the numbers are real — the backlog is down 9 lakh in a week!

What To Do If You're Still Waiting

  1. Check your e-verification status - Your return won't be processed until it's verified
  2. Make sure your bank account is pre-validated - This can cause delays at the last step
  3. Check for notices - Login to the e-filing portal and see if there are any pending actions
  4. Be patient (but hopeful) - The numbers are moving in the right direction

Pro tip: If it's been more than 60 days since you e-verified and still no refund, raise a grievance on the e-Nivaran portal. But given the current pace, you might want to wait another week or two first.
