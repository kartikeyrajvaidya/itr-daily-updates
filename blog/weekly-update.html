TL;DR

  • Processing has slowed sharply — only 51,643 returns cleared this week (vs 1.65 lakh last week)
  • Best single day this week: Mar 5 (Wed) with +19,727

The Numbers Don't Lie

Processing fell off a cliff this week. After settling at a moderate 30K–47K/day pace in late February, the IT Department shifted to a very slow 5K–20K/day rhythm. Here's the day-by-day picture for the week of Mar 1–7:

The week total: 51,643 returns processed — barely a third of last week's 1.65 lakh. The weekday average dropped to just ~10,300/day, the slowest pace since January. More alarmingly: new e-verifications (54,043 this week) outpaced processing (51,643) for the first time since the February burst began. The backlog has started growing again.

When Will Everyone Get Their Refund?

With the backlog now growing, projections have worsened since last week:

Optimistic (another burst like Feb 14 returns):

27.7 lakh ÷ 70,000/day = ~40 days → mid-April 2026

Moderate (late-Feb pace of ~30K/day):

27.7 lakh ÷ 30,000/day = ~92 days → early June 2026

Conservative (current March pace of ~10K/day continues):

27.7 lakh ÷ 10,000/day = ~277 days → late 2026 (unrealistic — a burst is almost certain)

The realistic range remains mid-April to early June. The current slow pace is almost certainly temporary — the IT Department has consistently used burst processing to clear backlogs. But the longer the slow patch continues, the more pressure builds.

For the first time since early February, processing (51,643) trailed new verifications (54,043). The ratio has flipped — the IT Department is now clearing returns 0.96x the rate new ones are entering the queue. The backlog grew by ~2,400 this week. This is a warning sign if the pattern continues into next week.

What To Do If You're Still Waiting

  1. Check your e-verification status - Your return won't be processed until it's verified
  2. Make sure your bank account is pre-validated - This can cause delays at the last step
  3. Check for notices - Login to the e-filing portal and see if there are any pending actions
  4. Be patient - The queue is clearing, just more slowly than mid-February suggested

Pro tip: If it's been more than 60 days since you e-verified and still no refund, raise a grievance on the e-Nivaran portal. With the current moderate pace, don't wait too long.

What We're Watching Next Week

  • Will processing pick back up to 30K+/day, or does the current 10K/day pace continue?
  • Can the backlog reverse course below 27.6 lakh again?
  • March 31 is the advance tax due date — will that pull IT Department attention away from ITR processing?
  • With the filing season closing in (July 2026 deadline), are older FY 2024-25 returns getting priority?

The February surge cleared a remarkable ~23.7 lakh returns. But the momentum has stalled. At just 51,643 returns this week — and the backlog growing for the first time in a month — the situation needs to be watched closely. We update daily on the dashboard.
