7 Best NPS Calculators Online for 2026

We tested every popular NPS calculator for corpus accuracy, pension estimates, tax benefit info, and the 60-40 annuity split. Here's which ones retirement planners should trust.

February 26, 2026 9 min read Tool Comparison

The National Pension System (NPS) is one of India's most tax-efficient retirement tools — with up to ₹2 lakh in deductions (80CCD(1) + 80CCD(1B)) and a partially tax-free withdrawal at 60. But NPS is also one of the most confusing to plan for, because your final corpus depends on contribution amount, years of investing, expected returns, and the 60-40 lump sum/annuity split.

We tested 7 NPS calculators with the same inputs — ₹5,000/month from age 25 to 60, 10% expected return, 6% annuity rate — and compared their results. For 35 years of monthly investing, the correct total corpus is approximately ₹1.14 crore, with a tax-free lump sum of ₹68.4 lakh and estimated monthly pension of ₹22,800.

Quick Verdict

  • Best overall: ITR Stats — real-time results, 60/40 split, pension estimate, interactive charts, year-by-year table
  • Most authoritative: NPS Trust — official calculator from PFRDA, definitive rules
  • Best for tax planning: ClearTax — NPS calculator integrated with 80CCD deduction planning
  • Best for fund performance: Groww — shows actual NPS fund returns alongside calculator

What We Tested

For each calculator, we checked:

  • Formula accuracy — correct monthly compounding over 20-35 year periods
  • 60/40 split — shows lump sum (60%, tax-free) and annuity (40%) breakdown
  • Pension estimate — estimates monthly pension from annuity portion
  • Tax benefit info — mentions 80CCD(1), 80CCD(1B), 80CCD(2) deductions
  • Charts and breakdown — visual corpus growth, invested vs returns split
  • Age-based inputs — current age + retirement age (not just years)
  • Real-time calculation — updates as you type

#2
NPS Trust (Official)
npstrust.org.in
Official

The official NPS calculator from PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority). Being the regulatory body's own tool, it uses the definitive rules for NPS corpus calculation. However, the interface is dated, and it doesn't provide the visual breakdowns that third-party tools offer.

Strengths

  • Official PFRDA tool — authoritative
  • Correct by definition
  • Both Tier 1 and Tier 2 support
  • Employer contribution field

Limitations

  • Dated, clunky interface
  • No charts or visual breakdown
  • No pension estimate
  • No year-by-year growth table
  • Slow page load
Best for: Cross-checking your NPS corpus calculation against the official source. Use as a verification tool.

Strengths

Limitations
