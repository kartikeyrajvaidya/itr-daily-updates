7 Best Lumpsum Calculators Online for 2026

We tested every popular lumpsum calculator for formula accuracy, growth multiplier, charts, and year-by-year breakdown. Here's which ones one-time investors should use.

February 26, 2026 8 min read Tool Comparison

Received a bonus, inheritance, or maturity payout? A lumpsum calculator tells you what that one-time investment will grow to. The math is simple — compound interest: FV = P × (1 + r)^n — but the visualisation and context matter. ₹5 lakh at 12% for 20 years becomes ₹48.23 lakh — a 9.65x multiplier. Seeing that growth curve year by year changes how you think about investing.

We tested 7 lumpsum calculators with identical inputs — ₹1,00,000 at 12% for 10 years. The correct answer is ₹3,10,585 (a 3.11x multiplier).

Quick Verdict

  • Best overall: ITR Stats — real-time results, growth multiplier, interactive charts, year-by-year table, dark mode
  • Best for fund investing: Groww — SIP/Lumpsum toggle with direct fund investment
  • Best for tax planning: ClearTax — ELSS lumpsum with tax-saving integration
  • Best for goal planning: ET Money — goal-based investment recommendations

What We Tested

For each calculator, we checked:

  • Formula accuracy — uses FV = P × (1 + r)^n correctly
  • Growth multiplier — shows how many times your money grows (e.g., 3.11x)
  • Year-by-year breakdown — table showing value at end of each year
  • Charts — doughnut (invested vs returns) and bar chart (growth timeline)
  • SIP comparison — shows lumpsum vs SIP for the same amount
  • Real-time calculation — updates as you type
  • Tax information — LTCG/STCG implications mentioned
