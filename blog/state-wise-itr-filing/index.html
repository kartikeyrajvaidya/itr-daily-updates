Key Insights

Why Maharashtra Leads

Maharashtra is home to Mumbai, India's financial capital, with the highest concentration of corporate headquarters, banks, and the stock exchange. The state also has a large industrial base in Pune, Nagpur, and other cities.

Gujarat's Strong Position

Despite having less than half of UP's population, Gujarat files almost as many returns. This reflects its strong business culture, industrial activity, and higher per-capita income.

Northeast's Low Filings

The entire Northeast (7 states) files only 10.1 lakh returns - less than Chhattisgarh alone. This is due to lower urbanization, prevalence of agriculture (exempt income), and special tax exemptions under Section 10(26) for certain tribal areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which state files the most income tax returns in India?
Maharashtra files the most income tax returns with 1.12 crore (11.2 million) filings in FY 2024-25, accounting for 15.1% of all filings. This is followed by Uttar Pradesh (74.2 lakh) and Gujarat (71.3 lakh).
Which state has the highest ITR filing growth?
Telangana has the highest growth at 141% year-on-year. Filings increased from 10.2 lakh to 24.6 lakh. This is primarily due to improved data consolidation after the state bifurcation from Andhra Pradesh.
Which region of India files the most taxes?
Western India leads with 37.7% of all filings (2.79 crore), followed by North India at 27.1% (2 crore) and South India at 21.3% (1.58 crore). East India contributes 12.5% and Northeast just 1.4%.
How many ITRs are filed from Delhi?
Delhi (NCT) filed 36.6 lakh income tax returns in FY 2024-25, making it the 8th highest filing state/UT. Delhi accounts for 4.9% of all ITR filings in India.
Which state has the lowest ITR filings?
Among states, Mizoram has the lowest filings at just 8,635. Among UTs, Ladakh has the lowest at 431 filings. Lakshadweep filed 4,808 returns in FY 2024-25.
