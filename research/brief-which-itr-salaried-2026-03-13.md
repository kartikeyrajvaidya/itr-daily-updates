## 8. Content Plan: Fun, Interactive, High Quality

### 8.1 Interactive Elements (Choose One or Combine)

**Option A — "Quick check" (3 questions on the page)**  
- Q1: "Is your total income above Rs 50 lakh?" Yes → ITR-2. No → next.  
- Q2: "Do you have any capital gains (stocks, mutual funds, property sale) or are you a director?" Yes → ITR-2. No → next.  
- Q3: "More than one house property or foreign income?" Yes → ITR-2. No → ITR-1.  
- Implementation: Simple show/hide with radio buttons; result box: "You’re likely in **ITR-1 (Sahaj)**" or "You’re likely in **ITR-2**" + CTA "Confirm with the full 5-question tool →".

**Option B — Scenario picker (cards)**  
- Four cards: "Salary + FD/interest only" | "Salary + sold stocks/MF" | "Salary above 50 L" | "Director / 2nd house / foreign income."  
- User clicks one; expandable answer: "You file **ITR-1**" or "You file **ITR-2**" with one-line reason + link to full tool.

**Option C — Both**  
- Quick check first (fast path), then "Or see scenarios" with the four cards.  
- **Recommendation:** Option C for maximum engagement and time-on-page without building a full new tool.

### 8.2 Fun to Read

- **Opening:** One short paragraph. "March. Form 16 has landed. Your only real question: which ITR form do I file? For most salaried people it’s ITR-1 or ITR-2. Here’s how to know which — in plain English, with real numbers."
- **Scenario cards:** Use names and one-line situations.  
  - "Ramesh: Rs 12 L salary, FD interest, one house → **ITR-1**."  
  - "Priya: Salary + sold some mutual funds this year → **ITR-2**."  
  - "Amit: Rs 55 L salary, no capital gains → **ITR-2** (income >50 L)."  
  - "Sneha: Director in a startup + salary → **ITR-2**."
- **Tone:** Short sentences. No "It is important to note." Use "You" and "Your." One short data sentence per section (e.g. "42% of 8.5 Cr returns are ITR-1").
- **No emojis** (per blog rules). Use clear headings and bold for form names (ITR-1, ITR-2).

### 8.3 High Quality (SEO + UX)

- **Featured snippet:** H2 "Which ITR Form Do Salaried Employees File?" — first 40–50 words = direct answer (ITR-1 or ITR-2; one sentence each when to use).
- **FAQPage schema:** 5–8 questions; answers in plain text, no markdown in schema. Include: "Can I file ITR-1 if I have capital gains?" "Which ITR if salary above 50 lakh?" "Which ITR for director?" "What if I file wrong form?"
- **Data citations:** One stat per major section (filing mix, ITR-2 growth). Link to itr-form-comparison and itr1-decline.
- **Mobile:** Quick check and scenario cards must work on small screens (stacked, tappable).
- **CTA placement:** After quick answer, after scenario section, before FAQ. Same CTA: "Get your form in 5 questions" → `/guide/itr-form/`.
