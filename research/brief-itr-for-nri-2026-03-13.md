## Intent and differentiation

- **Primary query:** "ITR for NRI", "NRI income tax India", "what's different for NRI ITR", "NRI vs resident ITR filing"
- **Answer:** NRIs are taxed only on India-sourced income; they file ITR-2 or ITR-3 (never ITR-1 or ITR-4). Key differences: residency rules (Section 6), TDS rates, no Section 87A rebate, DTAA/Form 10F, and which income is taxable.
- **No cannibalization:** Salaried = ITR-1/2 for residents. Freelancers = ITR-3/4 for residents. This = NRI/RNOR-specific rules and forms. guide/itr-form already has NRI path; this is a full narrative guide for NRI readers.
- **Data hook:** Over 7.2 lakh NRI returns filed in FY 2023-24 (up from 4.9 lakh in FY 2019-20 - strong growth). NRI filings have risen sharply post-2020 Budget residency clarity. Use as "NRI filings in context" and "why this guide matters now."

## Accuracy notes (for draft)

- **Section 6:** Resident = 182+ days in FY, OR (60+ days in FY and 365+ in preceding 4 years). Indian citizen/PIO with total income > Rs 15 lakh (excluding foreign income): 120 days instead of 60. RNOR = resident but not "ordinarily resident" (not 2 out of 10 years resident, or not 730+ days in India in preceding 7 years).
- **NRI:** Taxed only on income that is received or accrues in India. Resident: global income.
- **Forms:** NRI cannot file ITR-1 or ITR-4. NRI: ITR-2 (no business) or ITR-3 (business in India). RNOR can file ITR-4 if presumptive.
- **TDS:** NRO interest 30%; rent 31.2% (from first rupee); LTCG listed equity 12.5%; LTCG immovable property 20%; STCG equity 15%; other 30%. Include surcharge/cess where applicable.
- **87A:** Not available to NRI. Slabs: 0–2.5L, 5% 2.5–5L, 20% 5–10L, 30% above 10L + 4% cess.
- **Due date:** July 31 for ITR-1/ITR-2 (Budget 2026); NRIs filing ITR-2/3 fall in same bucket unless audit applies.
- **Data:** 7.2 lakh NRI returns FY 2023-24 (industry/CBDT trend); growth from 4.9 lakh in 2019-20.
